The Origin of Mosques

In order to understand the beginning of the first mosque, it is necessary to go back to the beginning of the Islamic religion itself. When the Prophet Muhammad was in his early forties, he began to hear mysterious voices and visions. He found solitude in a cave on the outskirts of Mecca, where the angel Gabriel appeared to him, telling him that he was the messenger of God, and to share the news of the one true god with the rest of mankind. Before long, he had a small following, which included his cousin, Ali, the son of the uncle who raised him. However, they were persecuted by the rich and elite merchants, because of their belief in the Meccan pantheon of gods and goddesses. He and the other believers in the one true God migrated to the oasis of Yathrib, which they called Medina, meaning “the city of the prophet”. They lived there for ten years, slowly winning over converts. Muhammed’s home in Medina was the first mosque, and it is from his home that many mosques get certain elements of their design. Even at that first mosque, they prayed in the direction of the Kabba in Mecca. Eventually, a truce was made with the Meccans, and they were allowed to return as pilgrims, visiting the holy sights. But after one of his followers was murdered, he declared war on Mecca and quickly overtook the city. After their surrender, he made them destroy the pagan idols surrounding the Kabba. It was after the surrender of Mecca that his prestige grew greatly, and the religion and its mosques can now be found all over the world.
